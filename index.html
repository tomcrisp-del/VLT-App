Easy Gentle terrain with minimal elevation gain. Suitable for families with children, beginners, and those looking for a leisurely walk. Typically well-maintained paths with few obstacles. Usually less than a mile long.

Intermediate Moderate terrain with some hills and uneven footing. Requires a reasonable level of fitness. May include some steep sections or rougher ground. Good for hikers with basic trail experience. Usually less than two miles long.

Challenging Steep terrain, significant elevation gain, and potentially rough or technical footing. Requires good fitness and trail experience. May involve scrambling over rocks or navigating uneven ground. Usually two miles or longer.

Very Challenging Extremely steep sections, significant elevation gain, and difficult terrain. Best for experienced hikers in good physical condition. May include steep drops or exposed sections. May require kayak or small watercraft to access.

Many trails offer multiple loop options of varying difficulty, so you can often adjust the challenge level by choosing a shorter route.

TERRAIN FEATURES:

  • Steep - Significant elevation gain or downhill sections
  • Uneven Footing - Rocky, root-filled, or otherwise uneven ground
  • Wet Ground - Marshy areas, stream crossings, or muddy sections
  • Shaded - Mostly under tree cover (good in hot weather)
  • Sun Exposed - Open areas with little shade (bring sun protection)

HIGHLIGHTS & SCENIC FEATURES:

  • Scenic Views - Panoramic vistas or notable viewpoints
  • Shoreline - Direct access to water views or beaches
  • Beach Access - Sandy/stony beach suitable for walking
  • Waterfall - Cascading water feature
  • Quarry - Historic quarry site (from Vinalhaven's granite quarrying heritage)
  • Historical Sites - Historical sites or structures

AMENITIES:

  • Benches - Resting spots along the trail and potential picnic locations
  • Swimming - Safe water access for swimming

RESTRICTIONS:

  • No Dogs - Dogs not permitted on this trail. This is a STRICT rule.
  • Boat Access Only - Accessible only by private boat

Most Vinalhaven trails welcome dogs, but a few have restrictions. Properties with Sanctuary in the name, such as Starboard Rock Sanctuary and Whitmore Pond Sanctuary do not allow domesticated animals. Check each trail's feature tags before you go.

TIPS FOR HIKING WITH DOGS:

  • Keep dogs on leash where required by local regulations
  • Pack water for your dog
  • Watch for rough terrain that may hurt paws
  • Be mindful of wildlife - keep your dog close
  • Clean up after your dog on and off trail
  • Some trails have muddy sections - plan for cleanup
  • Consider your dog's fitness level for longer hikes

SAFETY FIRST:

  • Stay on marked trails - it's easy to get lost
  • Tell someone where you're going and when you'll return
  • Bring water and snacks, even for short hikes
  • Wear appropriate footwear with good traction
  • Check weather before heading out
  • Start early to ensure daylight to return
  • Bring a charged phone or GPS device
  • Use the buddy system - don't do long hikes alone, if possible

LEAVE NO TRACE PRINCIPLES:

  • Pack out all trash - leave no trace
  • Stay on designated trails to protect vegetation
  • Don't pick plants or flowers
  • Don't remove rocks, shells, or natural objects
  • Be quiet to avoid disturbing wildlife
  • Don't build cairns or rock stacks (except where marked)

TRAIL ETIQUETTE:

  • Yield to uphill hikers
  • Step aside for faster hikers
  • Control your dog at all times
  • Don't block trail with large groups
  • Respect private property at trailheads
  • Report trail hazards or damage to VLT Staff

Yes. Deer ticks (black-legged ticks) are present on Vinalhaven and can carry Lyme disease and other tick-borne illnesses. Ticks are most active from spring through late fall, especially in tall grass, brush, and wooded areas.

TICK SAFETY TIPS:

  • Wear long sleeves and light-colored clothing
  • Tuck pants into socks in high grass
  • Use insect repellent (DEET or picaridin recommended)
  • Stay in the center of trails when possible
  • Do a full tick check after your hike
  • Shower within two hours if possible
  • Check children and pets carefully

If you find an attached tick, remove it promptly with fine-tipped tweezers and monitor for symptoms. Contact your healthcare provider if you develop a rash or flu-like symptoms.

SPRING (May-June): Wildflowers bloom, warming weather, longer daylight hours, some wet areas from snowmelt, great for wildlife viewing.

SUMMER (July-September): Peak hiking season, warmest weather but can be foggy, beaches and swimming at their best, more visitors on popular trails. Morning and late afternoon hikes are best to avoid midday sun and heat. More biting insects.

FALL (October-November): Spectacular foliage, cooler weather, fewer biting insects, shorter daylight so start hikes early. Migrating birds and raptors visible. Wear blaze orange during hunting season (early September through mid-December).

WINTER (December-April): Quieter trails, shorter daylight hours (start early), potential ice and muddy sections, some trails may be difficult to navigate in deep snow. Beautiful but requires extra caution and equipment.

TIDES: Many Vinalhaven trails are affected by tides. Plan accordingly if your route includes shoreline sections. The Shore Trail at Tip Toe has a stone dam which is only passable outside of High Tide.

Most Vinalhaven trails have designated parking areas. Parking is free and available year-round, but can fill up during summer weekends. Parking overnight is not allowed at trailheads.

PARKING TIPS:

  • Many trailheads have limited parking
  • Arrive early, especially on weekends
  • Park respectfully in designated areas only
  • Some trails share parking with other properties

BOAT ACCESS:

  • Barton's Quarry Preserve is accessible only by private boat
  • Some island trails may require water access
  • Plan accordingly if your trail requires boat transportation

ACCESS INFORMATION:

  • All trails are open dawn to dusk
  • Some are managed by different organizations (VLT, MCHT, Town)
  • Properties are closed during hunting seasons in some cases

FOG: Fog is common, especially in summer mornings. Can reduce visibility significantly. Stay on marked trails if fog rolls in.

WIND: Island winds can be strong and gusty. Extra caution on cliffs and exposed areas. Wind chill can make it feel much colder in Winter and Spring.

TIDES: Some trails are affected by tide changes. Plan accordingly for shoreline sections. Check tide tables before your hike. Some areas only accessible at low tide.

RAIN: Sudden showers are common. Trails become muddy and slippery. Wear waterproof jacket and appropriate footwear. Stream crossings may be harder after rain. Exposed ledge can be exceptionally slippery when wet—exercise extreme caution.

LIGHTNING: Avoid ridge tops and exposed areas during storms. Don't shelter under isolated trees. Leave trail immediately if you see or hear lightning in the distance.

SEASONAL: Winter: Ice, snow, shorter daylight. Spring: Muddy sections from snowmelt. Summer: Heat, humidity, insects. Fall: Slippery leaves, shorter days.

ESSENTIALS (all hikes):

  • Water (2+ liters depending on distance)
  • Snacks or light food
  • Map or trail app with GPS
  • Fully charged phone
  • Appropriate footwear with good traction
  • Layers (weather changes quickly on islands)
  • Sun protection (sunscreen, hat, sunglasses)

RECOMMENDED:

  • First aid kit (blister treatment, bandages, pain relief)
  • Natural insect repellent (choose environmentally responsible options)
  • Flashlight or headlamp (for late starts or emergencies)
  • Whistle (for emergencies or in fog)
  • Small trash bag if bringing food items (leave no trace)
  • Camera or binoculars
  • Trekking poles (helpful on steep sections)

FOR LONGER HIKES (2+ hours):

  • Extra water and food
  • Waterproof jacket
  • Extra batteries/portable charger
  • Hygiene items (pack out all waste and toilet paper)

FOR SHORELINE/BEACH TRAILS: Waterproof bag, water shoes, towel, change of clothes, sunscreen.

FOR SWIMMING: Swimsuit, towel, change of clothes, waterproof bag, water shoes for rocky bottoms.

While Vinalhaven's naturally rocky and forested terrain limits fully accessible trails, several offer easier access:

EASIEST OPTIONS:

  • The Carrying Place Bridge Bench Trail is currently being transformed into a universally accessible trail, offering a short, wheelchair-accessible path to a beautiful waterfront vista with ample room for accessible parking. This project is expected to be completed by July of 2026.
  • Eleanor L. Campbell Preserve's Lower Parking Lot offers relatively flat sections and a lovely, secluded beach vista
  • Armbrust Hill Town Park - Some manageable areas close to the ICMS Building for those with mobility limitations
  • Skoog Park (Vinalhaven Land Trust Headquarters) offers a drive-up view of the Sands Cove and a short, universally accessible hardened path to picnic benches

ACCESSIBILITY CONSIDERATIONS:

  • Most Vinalhaven trails have natural, uneven terrain
  • No trails are currently wheelchair accessible due to uneven terrain
  • Most Vinalhaven trails have limited parking near trailheads
  • Uneven footing is common throughout the island
  • Some sections may be muddy or wet

TIPS FOR THOSE WITH MOBILITY CHALLENGES:

  • Choose trails rated "Easy" difficulty
  • Avoid trails with "Steep" or "Uneven Footing" tags
  • Start with shorter distances
  • Use trekking poles for extra stability
  • Bring a friend for support
  • Check trail conditions before going
  • Don't hesitate to turn back if it becomes too difficult

CONTACT ORGANIZATIONS: Vinalhaven Land Trust (VLT) - Can discuss specific trail access for all VLT, MCHT and Town trails and parks

Trail conditions change seasonally and after storms. Here's how to stay informed:

BEFORE YOU GO:

  • Check weather forecast
  • Review recent condition updates on the Resources Page of the VHTrails App
  • Ask Staff at the VLT Office (12 Skoog Park, Vinalhaven, ME) or call (207) 863-2543

HOW TO REPORT PROBLEMS:

  • Report hazards: significant downed trees, big recent erosion, or vandalism
  • Contact Staff at the VLT Office (12 Skoog Park, Vinalhaven, ME) or call (207) 863-2543
  • Use the Report a Problem Button on the VHTrails app
  • Note: broken signs, seriously overgrown sections, or water hazards
  • Provide specific location details
  • Photos are helpful

TRAIL MAINTENANCE:

  • VLT, MCHT, and Town maintain different properties
  • Volunteer work days are available throughout the year
  • Report maintenance needs to the appropriate organization

SEASONAL CLOSURES: Check before visiting in fall/winter. Heavy snow may make winter trails difficult or impassable. Spring mud season can be challenging.

Three organizations manage Vinalhaven's trail network:

VINALHAVEN LAND TRUST (VLT) - Teal logo

  • Non-profit focused on land conservation
  • Manages the majority of Vinalhaven trails
  • Works to preserve natural areas for public use
  • Website: vinalhavenlandtrust.org
  • Manages properties like: A.W. Smith Preserve, Basin Preserve, Eleanor Campbell, Granite Island, and many others

MAINE COAST HERITAGE TRUST (MCHT) - Blue logo

  • Statewide land trust focused on coastal conservation
  • Manages several Vinalhaven properties
  • Protects working forests and natural habitats
  • Website: mcht.org
  • Manages properties like: Carrying Place, Huber Preserve, Lane's Island, Williams Preserve, and others

TOWN OF VINALHAVEN - Gold/yellow logo

  • Municipal government managing town properties
  • Maintains town parks and recreation areas like quarries and skating rink (the old ball field)
  • Manages public access and facilities
  • Website: townofvinalhaven.org
  • Manages properties like: Armbrust Hill Town Park, State Beach, and Armbrust Hill

WHY THREE ORGANIZATIONS?

  • Each works to preserve land through different methods
  • Some properties are co-owned or managed jointly
  • Combination provides comprehensive trail network
  • All welcome public use and support conservation

GETTING INVOLVED:

  • All three organizations welcome volunteers
  • Attend trail maintenance work days
  • Support through donations
  • Join mailing lists for updates
  • Participate in conservation decisions

The Vinalhaven Land Trust's

Volunteer Program

Do you love our trails so much that you want to help maintain them? The OWLS are a welcoming group that keeps our island's preserves clear, safe, and thriving. Here's the idea: Walk your favorite trails anytime, spot what needs attention, and report it. Then join us on volunteer workdays where we tackle the big stuff together: building trail features, cutting new trails, repairing erosion, and more!

No experience? No problem. We provide all tools and gear, pair newcomers with experienced trail crew, and even offer professional chainsaw safety training for those ready to grow their skills. Every hour you give safeguards the future of trails you love. You'll build real skills, make lasting friendships, and see the difference your work makes every time you walk through. Spread your wings and join the crew!
